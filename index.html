Bienvenidos

Since 2010, El Viejo Almacén has brought the authentic spirit of Buenos Aires' traditional bodegones to San Juan's vibrant Calle Loiza.

Named after the famous tango house in Buenos Aires, our restaurant celebrates Argentinian culinary traditions influenced by Italian immigrants.

Owned by Adrián Romano, an Argentinian immigrant with over 22 years in the restaurant industry, every meal is a journey to the heart of Argentina.

Our Journey

From Buenos Aires to Calle Loiza

2010

The Beginning

Adrián Romano opens El Viejo Almacén, bringing the authentic bodegón experience to Puerto Rico.

2017

Resilience

After Hurricane Maria, Adrián and his team sell choripán on the streets to keep the dream alive.

2019

Expansion

Opening of second location in Guaynabo.

2020

Reimagined

Calle Loiza location reopens with a fresh, redesigned space at 1604 Calle Loiza.

Today

A Culinary Landmark

Continuing to thrive with authentic cuisine, live tango music, and traditional hospitality.

Chef Alfredo Juncos

From Córdoba, Argentina

Chef Alfredo Juncos brings authentic Argentinian flavors to every plate. His cooking is deeply rooted in Italian traditions that have shaped Argentinian cuisine.

Specialties include homemade pastas, traditional milanesas, expertly grilled parrilladas, and signature dishes like lamb shank braised in Malbec.
